Seasons

Seasons remind us that life is ever-changing—spring brings hope, summer joy, autumn reflection, and winter rest. In their rhythm, we find growth and beauty.

Spring 🌸

Eiffel Tower

📍 Location: Paris, France

🌤️ Best Seasons: Spring (Apr-Jun) & Autumn (Sep-Oct)

An iconic iron tower rising above Paris, offering breathtaking views of the city.

Built in 1889 for the World’s Fair, the Eiffel Tower is one of the most recognizable landmarks on Earth. Visitors can take elevators to observation decks for panoramic views of Paris. At night, the tower sparkles with thousands of lights every hour.

Great Wall

📍 Location: Northern China

🌤️ Best Seasons: Spring & Autumn

An ancient wall stretching thousands of kilometers across mountains and valleys.

The Great Wall was built over centuries to protect Chinese kingdoms from invasions. It stretches more than 21,000 km. Some of the most popular sections for visitors include Badaling and Mutianyu near Beijing.

Colosseum

📍 Location: Rome, Italy

🌤️ Best Seasons: Spring & Autumn

The largest ancient amphitheater ever built in the Roman Empire.

Completed in 80 AD, the Colosseum once hosted gladiator battles, animal hunts, and massive public spectacles. Today it stands as a powerful reminder of ancient Roman engineering and culture.

Directions

Patra

📍 Location: Southern Jordan

🌤️ Best Seasons: Spring & Autumn

An ancient city carved directly into red desert cliffs.

Petra was the capital of the Nabataean Kingdom over 2,000 years ago. Its most famous structure, the Treasury, is carved into sandstone cliffs and revealed dramatically through a narrow canyon called the Siq.

Grand Canyon

📍 Location: Arizona, USA

🌤️ Best Seasons: Spring & Autumn

A massive canyon carved by the Colorado River with breathtaking layered cliffs.

The Grand Canyon stretches 446 km long and over 1.6 km deep. Its colorful rock layers reveal millions of years of Earth’s history.

Winter ❄️

Taj Mahal

📍 Location: Agra, India

🌤️ Best Seasons: Winter (Nov-Feb)

A stunning white marble mausoleum known as a symbol of eternal love.

Commissioned by Emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century, the Taj Mahal was built in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. The structure blends Persian, Islamic, and Indian architecture and changes color throughout the day depending on the sunlight.
